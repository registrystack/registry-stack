Wallets hold or present evidence for a person or organization, while Registry Stack focuses on the
registry-side issuance, evaluation, metadata, and audit surfaces that can feed those wallet flows.

## Ecosystem role

A wallet may manage keys, receive credentials, request credentials, present credentials, or help a
holder consent to disclosure.
Those holder-side responsibilities are outside the Registry Stack boundary.

Registry Witness can issue configured evidence artifacts and credentials from registry-backed claim
evaluation.
Registry Platform supplies shared credential issuance and cryptographic primitives used by runtime
services.
Registry Manifest can publish evidence offering metadata so an ecosystem can discover what a
registry-backed issuer exposes.

## Where overlap exists

The overlap appears around credential offers, subject identifiers, holder proof, and disclosure
policy.
Registry Stack does not become a wallet.
It does not manage a holder's credential store, decide consent, or present credentials to relying
parties.

Registry Witness owns the server-side claim evaluation and issuance path.
The wallet owns holder interaction and presentation.
The relying party owns acceptance policy.

## Pattern

Use Registry Witness when a registry-backed issuer needs to return:

- A structured claim result for a service caller
- CCCEV-shaped evidence JSON-LD
- An SD-JWT VC credential
- An OpenID for Verifiable Credential Issuance (OID4VCI) credential issuance flow

Use wallet infrastructure for holder custody, presentation, and relying-party protocols that sit
outside the registry service boundary.

## Standards fit

SD-JWT VC and OID4VCI describe credential-shaped outputs and issuance flows.
W3C Decentralized Identifiers (DID Core) apply to the narrow DID method surfaces documented in the
runtime services.
CCCEV and JSON-LD apply when evidence needs a semantic evidence vocabulary rather than only a
credential envelope.
