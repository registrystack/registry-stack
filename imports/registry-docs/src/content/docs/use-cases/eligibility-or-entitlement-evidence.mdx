Use this page when a service needs to know whether a person, household, organization, or asset
meets a rule, but the service does not need the source registry record.
The pattern keeps the question narrow and makes the evidence artifact smaller than the record.

## Model

Registry Witness is the built evaluator and issuer.
It takes a subject identifier, reads configured source data, evaluates a configured rule, applies
the configured disclosure mode, and returns a signed evaluation or credential.

Registry Manifest can publish service, requirement, and evidence metadata that describes what
facts a consumer may request.
Registry Lab demonstrates a wallet-facing issuance flow for a specific lab scenario, but the
pattern does not require that every deployment use a wallet.

## Example facts

- The subject satisfies an age, residency, licensing, or registration rule.
- The subject has a valid entitlement at the evaluation time.
- The subject is associated with a registered asset or case.
- The source registry can confirm the fact without releasing the full case file.

These facts are examples of claim shape, not built-in policy.
Each deployment defines the rule, source binding, disclosure mode, credential format, and issuer
identity.

## Why this differs from record sharing

Traditional record sharing gives the relying service a copy of the source material and asks the
service to interpret it.
The Witness pattern evaluates the rule near the source and gives the relying service the result
the policy allows.

This reduces unnecessary disclosure, but it does not eliminate governance.
The issuing institution still owns the rule, audit trail, correction process, appeal process, and
terms under which the relying service may request the fact.

## Built and future-facing parts

Registry Witness already implements claim evaluation and issuance surfaces.
Registry Manifest already publishes requirement and evidence metadata used for discovery.

Future registry platform work may make more source systems available through common services.
That future work is separate from the current ability to issue a narrow evaluation or credential
from a configured source.
