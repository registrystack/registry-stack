## Starting situation

A land administration authority maintains a smallholder-plot registry covering 120,000 parcels
across six provinces.
A rural-credit scheme needs to confirm, before approving a loan, that the applicant holds a
registered plot title in their name.
The credit scheme needs a yes/no answer against a national land-parcel identifier.
It does not need the plot boundaries, transaction history, or co-ownership details.

## What you set up

Configure a Registry Witness claim for the title-holder fact.
The claim definition names the source binding (the parcel registry table), the rule (a valid
registered title exists for the subject identifier), the disclosure mode (predicate: true/false),
and the issuer identifier.

Registry Manifest can publish service, requirement, and evidence metadata that describes what
facts a consumer may request.
A consumer reads the evidence offering document to understand the subject identifier format,
the claim scope, and the issuer before making runtime calls.

Registry Lab demonstrates a wallet-facing issuance flow that fits this pattern, but a wallet is
not required.
The evaluation endpoint works without a wallet-based credential flow when the relying service
calls Witness directly.

## What a caller does

The credit-scheme workflow sends an authorized request to the Witness evaluation endpoint,
passing the national land-parcel identifier as the subject and naming the title-holder claim.

For a credential flow, the applicant receives an SD-JWT VC.
They present only the title-holder disclosure to the credit scheme, keeping co-ownership details
and plot-boundary fields in the unselected credential attributes.

## What the caller gets back

A signed evaluation containing: the subject identifier, the predicate result (title registered:
true or false), the evaluation timestamp, and the issuer identifier.

The plot record, boundaries, co-owner names, transaction history, and internal registry notes
do not appear in the response.

## What is in scope, what is not

This pattern covers: evaluating a narrow eligibility or entitlement rule near the source
registry, returning a predicate or signed credential, and publishing requirement and evidence
metadata for discovery.

The fact returned is narrower than the source record.
The issuing institution owns the rule, the audit trail, the correction process, and the terms
under which the relying service may request the fact.

This pattern does not cover: full record sharing, automatic determination of legal entitlement,
or credential wallet infrastructure.
Future registry platform work may make more source systems available through common services;
that work is separate from the current ability to evaluate a configured claim.
