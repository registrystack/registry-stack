Use this page when a relying party needs to know whether a business has a registry status, but
does not need the full business record.
The pattern is about business registry verification: incorporation status, active status,
registration class, or a similar narrow fact.

## Model

Registry Witness is the built component that evaluates configured claims and can return a signed
evaluation, a redacted result, or an SD-JWT VC credential.
Registry Relay can publish metadata that tells consumers where a relevant evidence offering lives.
Registry Manifest can publish business registry metadata using BRegDCAT-AP-shaped artifacts.

In this pattern, the relying party asks for a status fact.
The registry-facing service evaluates the configured rule against the source record and returns
only the allowed result.
The full business profile, ownership details, filings, addresses, or internal registry notes do
not need to move through the relying party workflow unless the governing policy permits them.

## Example facts

- The business registration exists.
- The registration status is active.
- The business is registered for a named category.
- The business identifier matches the issuing registry's namespace.
- The status was evaluated at a stated time by a stated issuer.

The exact fields are deployment-specific.
The common property is that each answer is narrower than the underlying business record.

## Disclosure choices

Registry Witness supports different disclosure modes for a configured claim.
A status fact can be returned as a value, a predicate, or a redacted result, depending on the
claim definition.
For credential flows, SD-JWT VC lets a holder present selected disclosures while keeping
unselected credential fields hidden.

The disclosure mode belongs in governance and claim configuration.
The implementation does not make the legal decision that a relying party is entitled to a full
record.

## Boundaries

The registry subject is a business, and the relying party is verifying a business registry fact
for an administrative, compliance, procurement, licensing, or commercial workflow.

This page describes built Witness, Relay, and Manifest surfaces.
It does not claim dynamic trust-chain discovery, open federation, or automatic legal-basis
approval.
